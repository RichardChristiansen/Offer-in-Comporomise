What is an Offer in Compromise?

An Offer in Compromise (OIC) is an agreement between a taxpayer and the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) that resolves a taxpayer's tax liability for less than the full amount owed. It's an option available to those who genuinely cannot pay their full tax debt.

The IRS carefully considers several factors when evaluating an OIC, including your ability to pay, your income, your expenses, and your equity in assets. If approved, you can settle your tax debt and get a fresh financial start.

Who Qualifies for an OIC?

Financial Hardship

You must prove that paying your tax debt in full would create a significant financial hardship for you.

Tax Compliance

You must have filed all required federal tax returns and have made all necessary estimated tax payments.

No Bankruptcy

You cannot be in an open bankruptcy proceeding when you submit your OIC application.
